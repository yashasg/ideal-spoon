## Output Summary

- **Position:** QLoRA is not the bottleneck for Hawaiian LLM training at our corpus scale (<50M tokens).
- **Dominant risks:** Tokenizer handling (ʻokina U+02BB, kahakō), register skew (Bible/OCR), data quality, eval methodology.
- **QLoRA gap measured:** ~0.1–0.5 PPL vs fp16 in literature; negligible vs upstream risks.
- **Failure modes to audit:** Catastrophic forgetting, register collapse, eval-set leakage — not quantization noise.

## Proposed A/B Tests

1. **Base vs QLoRA-DAPT** on held-out Hawaiian PPL, ʻokina/kahakō survival, English regression, chrF probe.
2. **QLoRA(r=64) vs LoRA-fp16(r=64) vs full FT** on 0.5B–1B smoke model (fp16 fits).
3. **Rank sweep** r ∈ {16, 32, 64, 128} at fixed NF4 to separate capacity from quantization.
4. **Tokenizer audit deltas** (tokens/word, byte-fallback rate, ʻokina survival) as co-variables.
5. **Embeddings/lm_head trainability** ablation under QLoRA — most "QLoRA can't learn diacritics" claims dissolve with unfrozen embedding rows.
6. **Human spot eval** N=20–50 on Stage 1, N=30/direction on Stage 2 — metrics alone unreliable at this scale.

## Recommendation

Don't reject QLoRA on priors; measure the quant gap empirically. At our scale, tokenizer and data-quality gaps dominate.
